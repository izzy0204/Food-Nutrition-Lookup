Many people with medical or sensory dietary restrictions cannot simply choose any available food item. Someone with ARFID may depend on a narrow range of acceptable foods, while someone with hypertension, diabetes, or kidney disease may need meals that fit specific nutritional limits. Existing methods such as searching restaurant nutrition pages or looking up individual foods online are often slow, fragmented, and focused on single items rather than complete meal choices. A system that can quickly identify both safe foods and safe meal combinations would make dietary decision-making more practical and accessible.

The Food Nutrition Lookup System reads a dataset of over 300,000 food items, all of which containing calories, protein, carbs, fat, sodium, and sugar. This program allows users to search for foods, flter foods based on nutritional value, ingredient exclusion, and allergen exclusion, and build simple 3 component meals depending on nutritional preferences, for example needing to hit a protein goal or only allowing x amount of carbs for the overall meal.

To start using this program, [download the OpenNutrition TSV file](https://www.opennutrition.app/download) separately and place it in the data folder:
data/opennutrition_foods.tsv
Then, run the CLI and choose from the options within the terminal.
